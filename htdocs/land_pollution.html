Land Pollution

Land pollution, according to Britannica, is the deposition of solid or liquid waste materials on land or underground in a manner that can contaminate the soil and groundwater, threaten public health, and cause unsightly conditions and nuisances. (Nathanson, J. A., N.D.)
However, the definition is not limited to adding harmful material to soil. It can also be caused by removal of beneficial or dormant materials from the ground, causing the balance to tilt, which results in different consequences.

Causes of Land Pollution

Land pollution can be caused by:

  • Solid Waste;
  • Chemical Waste;
  • Industrial and Construction Materials;
  • Deforestation;
  • Mining;
  • Natural Disasters and;
  • Littering


Effects of Land Pollution

Land pollution may cause the following:

  • Climate Change;
  • Health effects due to Contamination;
  • Soil erosion and;
  • Wildfires
