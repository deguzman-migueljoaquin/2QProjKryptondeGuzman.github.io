Water Pollution

Water Pollution, according to Britannica, is the release of substances into subsurface groundwater or into lakes, streams, rivers, estuaries, and oceans to the point where the substances interfere with beneficial use of the water or with the natural functioning of ecosystems. (Nathanson, J.A., N.D.)
Water is an important part of life. Contaminating it will not only affect us, but will affect all living creatures here on Earth.

Causes of Water Pollution

Water pollution can be caused by:

  • Natural Occurances;
  • Chemical Waste;
  • Industrial and Construction Materials;
  • Solid Waste;
  • Oil Spills;
  • Radioactive Waste and;
  • Littering


Effects of Water Pollution

Water pollution may cause the following:

  • Climate Change;
  • Health effects due to Contamination;
  • Disruption of every day life on Earth and;
  • Destruction of Ecosystems
