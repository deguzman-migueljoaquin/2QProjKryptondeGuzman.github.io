Air Pollution

Air Pollution, according to Britannica, is the release into the atmosphere of various gases, finely divided solids, or finely dispersed liquid aerosols at rates that exceed the natural capacity of the environment to dissipate and dilute or absorb them. (Nathanson, J. A., N.D.)
The air is essential because we need it to breathe. Almost all living things on Earth need air for them to survive. Polluting it will eventually lead to great consequences, just like Land and Water Pollution.

Causes of Air Pollution

Air pollution can be caused by:

  • Fine Solid Particles suspended in air;
  • Aerosols;
  • Waste released from vehicles and houses;
  • Natural occurances, like volcanoes, and;
  • Radioactive decay


Effects of Air Pollution

Air pollution may cause the following:

  • Health effects and many diseases;
  • Acid rain;
  • Global Warming;
  • Depletion of the ozone layer and;
  • Disruption of every day life on Earth
